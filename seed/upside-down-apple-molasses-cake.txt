= Upside Down Apple Molasses Cake

# Preheat oven to 350 degF.

# Melt butter in a 10-inch cast-iron or other oven-proof skillet; set aside.

- 5 tablespoons Butter

# Whisk together all dry ingredients in a medium bowl.

- 1 3/4 cups All-Purpose Flour
- 1 1/2 teaspoons Salt
- 1 teaspoon Ground Cinnamon
- 3/4 teaspoon Baking Soda
- 1/2 teaspoon Baking Powder

# In a large bowl, whisk together molasses, egg, ginger, and sugar. Whisk in sour cream, then milk.

- 3/4 cup Unsulphured Molasses
- 1 each Large Egg
- 2 teaspoons Fresh Ginger, peeled and grated
- 1/4 cup Granulated Sugar
- 1/3 cup Sour Cream
- 1/4 cup Whole Milk

# Gradually whisk dry ingredients into wet ingredients, then whisk in 3 tablespoons of the melted butter from the skillet. Set batter aside.

# Add the remaining sugar to the butter left in the skillet. Cook over medium-high heat until sugar begins to caramelize, 2-3 minutes. Do not stir, but swirl the butter and sugar in the pan to melt and start turning color.

- 1/4 cup Granulated Sugar

# Add apples and turn to coat with sugar and butter. Cook on rounded side for about 3 minutes, adjusting heat as needed. Turn apples over and cook until they begin to soften, about 5 minutes longer.

- 3-4 each Crisp Apples

# Space apples evenly in skillet and pour cake batter over them.

# Bake in oven until a toothpick inserted into center comes out with a few moist crumbs, 30-40 minutes.

# Cool cake in skillet for 10 minutes, then carefully invert onto a plate. Serve warm or at room temperature with ice cream or whipped cream.

# For cupcakes. Add 1/2 cup finely grated and chopped carrot. Omit apples. Bake at 400 degF for 5 minutes, reduce heat to 350 degF and bake an additional 13-15 minutes.

= Posole Rojo (Pork and Hominy Stew)

# Preheat the broiler and set the oven rack about 6 inches from the heat source. Line a baking sheet with foil. Place poblano chiles cut-side down on the sheet.

- 2 each Poblano Chile Peppers

# Cook under the broiler until the skins blacken and blister, about 5 minutes. Transfer to a bowl and cover tightly with plastic wrap. Let steam and cool for 20 minutes. Remove skins and discard. Dice chiles.

# Cook bacon in a large pot over medium heat until it releases fat and begins to crisp, about 5 minutes. Drain on a paper towel-lined plate. Discard all but 1 tablespoon of bacon grease.

- 8 ounces Bacon, chopped

# Increase heat to medium-high. Add pork and cook, stirring occasionally, until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes. Remove pork and reduce heat to medium.

- 2 pounds Pork Shoulder, cut into 1-inch cubes

# Add onion and garlic to the pot. Cook, stirring, until softened and beginning to brown, about 5 minutes.

- 1 each Onion, diced
- 10 each Garlic Cloves, minced

# Stir in roasted poblano chiles, jalapeño peppers, oregano, cumin, chile powder, cloves, salt, and cilantro. Cook for 1 minute.

- 2 each Jalapeño Peppers, seeded and diced
- 1 teaspoon Mexican Oregano
- 2 teaspoons Ground Cumin
- 1 teaspoon New Mexico Chile Powder
- 1/4 teaspoon Ground Cloves
- 1/2 teaspoon Kosher Salt
- 1 bunch Cilantro, chopped

# Pour in chicken stock and enchilada sauce. Stir in bacon and pork. Bring to a simmer over medium-high heat. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer for 1 hour.

- 8 cups Chicken Stock
- 1 can (14 1/2 ounces) Mild Red Enchilada Sauce

# Stir in hominy, cover, and continue cooking for 1 hour until pork is very tender.

- 2 cans (29 ounces) White Hominy, rinsed and drained

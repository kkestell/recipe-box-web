= Banana Nut Breakfast Bars

# Adjust oven rack to lower-middle position and heat to 350 Â°F. Grease an 8- or 9-inch square pan and line with parchment paper covering bottom and two sides.

# In a large bowl, mash bananas with a fork until you have about 1 cup mashed bananas. Add eggs and whisk to combine. Set aside for 15 minutes.

- 2 each ripe medium bananas (about 240 grams peeled)
- 2 each large eggs

# Add olive oil, peanut butter, honey, salt, cinnamon, and vanilla to banana mixture. Whisk until smooth. Add oats and whisk to combine.

- 6 tablespoons (90 milliliters) olive oil, plus more for greasing
- 3/4 cup (187 grams) peanut or almond butter
- 6 tablespoons (120 grams) honey
- 2 teaspoons kosher salt, or 1 teaspoon fine salt
- 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- 1 cup (100 grams) old-fashioned rolled oats

# Scrape mixture into prepared pan. Top with walnuts and sprinkle with flaky salt.

- 3/4 cup (80 grams) walnut halves and pieces, roughly chopped
- Flaky salt, for topping

# Bake until top feels set when pressed lightly and toothpick inserted in center comes out clean, 35 to 40 minutes.

# Cool completely before using parchment to lift bars out of pan. Cut into 9 squares. Store in airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days.

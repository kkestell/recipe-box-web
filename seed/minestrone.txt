= Minestrone

# Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion, carrots, celery, and garlic. Saut√© until vegetables are softened, about 5 minutes.

- 2 tablespoons Extra-Virgin Olive Oil
- 1 cup Yellow Onion, diced
- 1 cup Carrots, diced
- 1 cup Celery, diced
- 4 each Garlic Cloves, minced

# Stir in Italian seasoning, salt, pepper, thyme, chili flakes, and tomato paste. Cook for 1 minute.

- 1 tablespoon Italian Seasoning
- 1 teaspoon Salt
- 1/2 teaspoon Fresh Ground Pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon Dried Thyme
- 1/4 teaspoon Chili Flakes
- 1 can (6 ounces) Tomato Paste

# Add chicken broth, vegetable broth, diced tomatoes, red wine vinegar, Parmesan rind, and the bay leaf. Bring to a simmer and cook for 20-40 minutes or until vegetables are tender.

- 4 cups Chicken Broth
- 3 cups Vegetable Broth
- 1 can (14 1/2 ounces) Diced Tomatoes
- 2-3 teaspoons Red Wine Vinegar
- 1 each Parmesan Rind
- 1 each Bay Leaf

# Meanwhile, cook pasta according to package directions. Before draining, set aside 1/4 cup of the pasta water. Briefly rinse pasta and set it aside. Ditalini, tubetti, conchiglie piccole, and orzo are good choices.

- 1 1/3 cups Pasta

# Add reserved pasta water, kidney beans, green beans, and zucchini. Stir to combine. Bring back to a simmer and cook for 10 minutes.

- 1/4 cup Pasta Water
- 1 can (15 1/2 ounces) Kidney Beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 cup Green Beans, frozen
- 1 cup Zucchini, diced

# Stir in chopped spinach and cook for an additional 5 minutes until wilted. Remove bay leaf before serving.

- 3 cups Spinach, chopped

# Serve soup over pasta and garnish with grated Parmesan cheese and fresh parsley.
